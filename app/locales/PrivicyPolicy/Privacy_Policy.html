COVID-RD App Privacy Policy

By registering and using the services of the COVID-RD application of the High Level Commission for the Prevention and Control of Coronavirus, YOU consent and accept that the treatment of your personal data is carried out in accordance with what is reported in this document and with the provided by the legislation in force in the Dominican Republic regarding the protection of personal data.

The High-Level Commission for the Prevention and Control of Coronavirus knows that you care about how your personal information is used and how it is shared and we thank you for trusting us to do so with care and prudence. This Privacy Policy describes the policies of the COVID-RD app and the procedures on the collection, transmission, manipulation, storage, disclosure and treatment of your information when you access our said app.

This App is operated and controlled by the Ministry of Public Health, which is responsible for the treatment for the purposes of Law No. 172-13 of December 15, 2013, which aims at the comprehensive protection of personal data established in files, public registers, data banks or other technical means of data processing for reporting purposes, whether public or private.

  1. Data processing

The personal data collected and accessed for registration in the application, including those declared by the user in the monitoring functionality, will be processed by the Ministry of Public Health for the purpose of surveying and containment related to the Coronavirus pandemic (COVID-19) and communicated within the framework of the provisions of the High Level Commission for the Prevention and Control of Coronavirus, related to the health emergency.

The COVID-RD App collects the following types of information:

The personal data collected in the symptom reporting process is found in a database managed by the Epidemiology Directorate of the Ministry of Public Health. The Ministry of Public Health has implemented security policies, standards and technical measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access or modification, misuse or disclosure, illegal destruction or accidental loss. To protect the data we store, we regularly audit your systems to detect possible vulnerabilities and attacks. However, since the Internet is not a one hundred percent secure environment, we cannot ensure or guarantee the security of any information that you transmit through the use of the APP or the guarantee that your information cannot be accessed, revealed, altered or destroy for breach of any of our physical, technical or management security measures. In the event that personal information is compromised as a consequence of a security breach, the Ministry of Public Health will notify persons whose personal information has been compromised as soon as it becomes aware of it.

In the case of personal data of positive COVID-19 users who give their consent to participate in contact tracing or Contact Tracing, such data is temporarily stored in a database managed by the Ministry of Economy, Planning and Development (MEPYD) and are only used to validate if there is a laboratory test in your name with a positive result. This is done to prevent false reports in Contact Tracing. After validation is done, that personal information is removed. In the case of geolocation data, these are transmitted completely anonymously to the server (it only indicates latitude, longitude, date and time). We use anonymization techniques that effectively allow anonymization of personal data so that it cannot be linked to a specific person. This anonymous transmission of the geolocation of the positives is carried out until the application determines through validation with the Ministry of Public Health that the user has received her epidemiological discharge or she decides to deactivate Contact Tracing.

The duly dissociated data may be used by the Ministry of Public Health for strictly statistical purposes.

The Contact Tracing module of the application is based on the Safe Paths protocol developed by the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), whose code was released by them and incorporated into the COVID-RD App to perform this function.

The information provided by the user as a consequence of the use of the app will be kept for two months, which once elapsed will proceed to delete said information from the servers.

Our Privacy Policy may change in order to adapt it to our practices. Any changes we make to our Privacy Policy in the future will be published on this page. We may email periodic reminders of our policies, but you are the one to check for changes. Unless otherwise stated, our current Privacy Policy applies to all information we have about you and your account. The Ministry of Public Health will not materially change our policies and practices to make them less protective of the information of Users collected in the past without their consent.

  1. Exercise of rights

You can exercise your rights of access, rectification, updating, inclusion and deletion, before the Epidemiology Directorate of the Ministry of Public Health by sending an email to Info@ministeriodesalud.gob.do, through the website https://coronavirusrd.gob.do/, or by phone * 462.

This Policy is governed in all its clauses and without exception by the laws of the Dominican Republic, especially, Law No. 172-13, Law No. 172-13 of December 15, 2013, which aims at comprehensive protection. of personal data stored in files, public registers, databases or other technical means of data processing intended to give reports, whether public or private.
